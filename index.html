about me

I am a graduate student in the Computer Science Department at Johns Hopkins University. I work with Dr. Ilya Shpitser affiliated with the Malone Center for Engineering in Healthcare.

Before joining Hopkins, I worked at Cummins Inc. and Tata Consultancy Services Ltd. as an I.T. Analyst for almost 5 years. I received my Bachelors in Technology in Instrumentation and Control Engineering from Bharati Vidyapeth College of Engineering, Delhi.

research

My main areas of interest are Causal Inference and Machine Learning, mostly involving probabilistic graphical models. On a broad level my interests lie in Artificial Intelligence, Cognitive Science and Philosophy of Science.

projects


Causal Identification when True Graph Structure is not known

Working in collaboration with Dr. Frederick Eberhardt on developing a method to identify causal effects from observational data without assuming a unique true causal structure.


asl-a: American Sign Language Assistant

A software package to convert ASL gestures to text/audio. The aim of the project is to create a system using which people with speech impairments can easily communicate with people who do not know ASL. We have implemented gesture recognition and conversion for the 26 alphabets for the english language. Currently we are working on adding simple phrases.


Evolution of Variational Inference

A review study of the evolution from Classical Variational Inference(2003) to Automatic Differentiation Variational Infernce(2015)


MLaaS: Machine Learning as a Service for exploring large datasets
